Great observation — and no, `H/9 * * * *` is **not** causing your failures. That's fine to use. Here's the concept:

---

**`*/9 * * * *`** means:
> Run at **exactly** minute 0, 9, 18, 27, 36, 45, 54 — on **every single job** that uses this schedule.

The problem with this in Jenkins is that if you have 50 jobs all set to `*/9 * * * *`, they all wake up at the **exact same second** and hammer the server simultaneously.

---

**`H/9 * * * *`** means:
> Run every 9 minutes, but pick a **random-but-consistent minute offset** based on a hash of the job name.

The `H` stands for **Hash**. Jenkins hashes your job name to pick a number, so:
- `copy-logs` might run at minute 3, 12, 21, 30...
- Another job might run at minute 7, 16, 25, 34...

They're still both running every 9 minutes — just **staggered** so they don't all collide.

---

**Simple analogy:**

Imagine 20 people all set alarms for 9:00 AM and they all try to get through the same door at once. `H` is Jenkins saying — *"I'll quietly assign you 9:03, and you 9:07, so you don't all crash into each other."*

---

**Bottom line for your task:**

| Expression | Meaning | Use it? |
|---|---|---|
| `*/9 * * * *` | Every 9 min, exact sync | Works but Jenkins discourages it |
| `H/9 * * * *` | Every 9 min, staggered | ✅ What Jenkins recommends |

Use `H/9 * * * *` as Jenkins suggests — it satisfies the task requirement of "every 9 minutes" and is actually the more correct DevOps practice.
