When you run sudo systemctl restart sample.service, it restarts the service, but it doesn't show you the detailed logs or errors from that restart.

journalctl -u sample.service is specifically for viewing the logs related to the sample.service, including any errors or warnings encountered during startup or runtime.

Think of systemctl as the command to control the service, and journalctl as the step to peek behind the curtain and see what happened under the hood. ðŸ˜„
